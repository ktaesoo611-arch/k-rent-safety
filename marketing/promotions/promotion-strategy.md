# Promotion Strategy & Guidelines

## Platform-Specific Approach

### Facebook Groups
**Tone:** Friendly, conversational, helpful
**Best Practice:** Focus on community value, not self-promotion
**Timing:** Post during weekday evenings (7-9 PM KST) or weekend mornings (9-11 AM KST)

### Reddit
**Tone:** Informative, humble, value-first
**Best Practice:** Engage authentically, provide expertise in comments
**Timing:** Weekday mornings (7-9 AM KST) or evenings (6-8 PM KST)

---

## Week 1 Posting Schedule

### Day 1-2: Reddit Focus
- Post to r/Living_in_Korea (most targeted)
- Monitor and respond to all comments

### Day 3-4: Facebook Groups
- Post to 2-3 most relevant expat groups
- Space out posts by 12+ hours

### Day 5-6: Expand Reddit
- Post to r/korea (if first post went well)
- Post to r/seoul with [Housing] flair

### Day 7: Additional Coverage
- Post to r/teachinginkorea
- Post to remaining Facebook groups
- Engage with all previous posts

## Engagement Strategy

### First 2 Hours After Posting:
- Stay online and respond to comments immediately
- Answer questions thoroughly
- Thank people for positive feedback
- Address concerns professionally

### Ongoing (First 24-48 Hours):
- Check every 2-4 hours
- Continue engaging with new comments
- Add valuable insights beyond the article
- Consider mentioning the Jeonse Safety Checker tool naturally if relevant

### After 48 Hours:
- Check daily for a week
- Engage with late comments
- Track which posts perform best

## Red Flags to Avoid

### Facebook:
- ❌ Posting to multiple groups within 1 hour
- ❌ Copy-pasting identical content
- ❌ Ignoring comments
- ❌ Being overly salesy

### Reddit:
- ❌ Using excessive emojis
- ❌ Posting to unrelated subreddits
- ❌ Defending your post aggressively
- ❌ Spamming across subreddits too quickly
- ❌ Not participating in the community beyond your post

---

## Measuring Success

Track the following:

### Engagement Metrics:
- Number of comments/questions
- Upvotes/reactions
- Shares
- Click-throughs to Medium (check Medium stats)

### Quality Indicators:
- Depth of questions asked
- People asking for the safety checker tool
- Positive feedback
- People sharing with others

### Platform Performance:
- Which Facebook groups had best engagement
- Which Reddit posts got most traction
- Best posting times for your content

---

## Follow-Up Actions

### If Post Goes Well:
- Thank engaged commenters
- Consider doing a follow-up Q&A post
- Share success stories (if people report back)
- Create additional related content

### If Post Gets Negative Feedback:
- Don't get defensive
- Ask for constructive feedback
- Improve the article based on legitimate concerns
- Wait before reposting to other communities

### If Post Gets Removed:
- Read the rules again carefully
- Message moderators politely to ask why
- Adjust content to comply
- Don't repost immediately

## Crisis Management

### If You Get Accused of Spam:
1. Acknowledge politely
2. Explain your genuine intent to help
3. Stop posting for a few days
4. Engage more in community discussions
5. Don't mention your article for a while

### If Someone Points Out Errors:
1. Thank them publicly
2. Verify the information
3. Update the Medium article
4. Post correction in comments
5. Show appreciation for feedback

### If Discussion Goes Off-Topic:
1. Gently redirect to helpful discussion
2. Don't engage with trolls
3. Report if needed
4. Stay professional

## Remember:

**Value First, Promotion Second**
- Be genuinely helpful
- Share knowledge freely
- Build credibility over time
- The article is a resource, not a sales pitch

**Be Patient**
- Organic growth takes time
- Quality engagement > quantity
- Build reputation in communities
- Results compound over time

**Stay Authentic**
- Share real experiences
- Admit what you don't know
- Ask questions too
- Be part of the community
