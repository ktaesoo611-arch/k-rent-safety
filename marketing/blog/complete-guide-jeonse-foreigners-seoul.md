## What is Jeonse? Understanding Korea's Unique Rental System

Jeonse (Ï†ÑÏÑ∏) is a unique Korean housing rental system where tenants pay a large lump-sum deposit (typically 50-80% of the property's market value) instead of monthly rent. At the end of the lease term (usually 2 years), the landlord returns the full deposit.

**How it works:**
- You pay a deposit ranging from ‚Ç©10 million to ‚Ç©500 million+ (roughly $8,000 to $400,000+)
- You live rent-free for 2 years (no monthly payments)
- The landlord uses your deposit as investment capital
- After the lease ends, you get 100% of your deposit back

**Why landlords offer jeonse:**
Landlords finance the acquisition of property without bank loan:
- no interest paymnet required
- leveraging more than bank loan

**Why tenants choose jeonse:**
- No monthly rent burden
- Lower total housing cost over 2 years compared to monthly rent (wolse)
- Builds housing stability
- Common practice in Korea, so more options available

## What Happens If Things Go Wrong?

### Scenario 1: Landlord Won't Return Deposit

**Steps:**
1. Send certified mail (ÎÇ¥Ïö©Ï¶ùÎ™Ö) demanding deposit return

**Your rights:**
- As a registered leaseholder with confirmation date stamp, you have priority claim
- You're ranked by confirmation date (earlier = higher priority)
- You can claim deposit from auction proceeds

**Priority order:**
1. Taxes and government fees

### Scenario 3: Need to Break Lease Early

**Standard contract:**
- Early termination usually requires landlord's consent
- You may forfeit part of deposit (10-30%)
- You must find replacement tenant

**How to minimize loss:**
1. Negotiate early termination clause in original contract
2. Find replacement tenant yourself (post in Facebook groups)
3. Offer to forfeit 1-2 months of equivalent rent
4. Time it with lease renewal season (Jan-Feb, Jul-Aug)
